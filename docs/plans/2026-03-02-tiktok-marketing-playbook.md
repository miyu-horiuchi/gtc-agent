# TikTok Slideshow Marketing Playbook — Beauty/Fashion App

## 1. The Slideshow Format

TikTok photo carousels (slideshows) outperform video for app marketing:
- 2.9x more comments, 2.6x more shares than video
- Easy to produce consistently — no filming, editing, or on-camera talent
- AI image generation makes them scalable (one person can produce 3+/day)

### The 6-Slide Structure

1. **Hook slide** — stops the scroll ("My friend said this filter looks fake... so I tested it")
2. **Setup** — establishes context/problem
3. **Discovery** — introduces your app naturally
4. **Result 1** — show it working
5. **Result 2** — build on the wow factor
6. **CTA slide** — drive the download ("search [App Name] on the App Store")

Every slide gets a text overlay — white bold text with black outline, positioned in the upper third. Reactions, not labels ("Wait... is this actually me??" not "AI Enhanced Photo").

## 2. Hook Formulas for Beauty/Fashion

Hooks are everything — if slide 1 doesn't stop the scroll, nobody sees slides 2-6.

### Person + Conflict (highest performing)
- "My boyfriend said I don't need makeup... so I showed him this"
- "My mom said this filter looks nothing like me"
- "My friend bet me this app couldn't fix her skin"

### POV / First Person
- "POV: you find an app that actually matches your foundation shade"
- "POV: your skincare routine just got replaced by AI"

### Listicle / Ranking
- "3 AI beauty apps I tested so you don't have to"
- "I ranked every filter app and this one won"

### Before/After Bait
- "I tried this app on my worst skin day"
- "What my face looks like at 7am vs what this app does to it"

### Challenge / Dare
- "Use this app on a photo with ZERO makeup. I dare you."
- "Try this on your most unflattering selfie"

### Rules for hooks
- Always involve a *person* and *tension* — conflict drives curiosity
- Conversational tone, like you're texting a friend
- 4-8 words on the slide, full story in the caption
- Test 2-3 hook categories per week, double down on what works, drop what doesn't

## 3. Image Generation & Text Overlays

### Generating slide images

Use any AI image model (Gemini, OpenAI, Stability, etc.). The model matters less than the prompt quality.

**Prompt formula:**
```
iPhone photo of [specific subject], [specific style], realistic lighting,
natural colors, taken on iPhone 15 Pro. No text, no watermarks, no logos.
```

**For beauty/fashion specifically:**
- Lock the face, skin tone, hair, and lighting across all 6 slides
- Always generate portrait (1024x1536 / 9:16) — fills the TikTok screen
- Be extremely specific: "close-up of a woman with medium brown skin, soft freckles, messy bun, no makeup, morning light from a bedroom window" beats "a pretty face"

**What makes images look real vs obviously AI:**
- "iPhone photo" + "realistic lighting" in every prompt
- Include imperfections — slightly messy hair, one earring, a coffee stain
- Lock the environment across slides (same room, same mirror, same lighting)
- Never use generic prompts like "a beautiful woman"

### Text overlays

Use Canva (free), CapCut, or any image editor.

- White bold text, thick black outline
- Upper third of the image (28-30% from top)
- 4-6 words per line, 3-4 lines max per slide
- Bottom 20% off-limits (TikTok UI covers it)
- Top 10% off-limits (status bar)
- Reactions not labels: "Wait... is this real??" not "AI Enhanced Photo"

## 4. Posting Strategy

### Schedule (3x/day minimum)
- **7:30 AM** — early scrollers
- **4:30 PM** — afternoon break
- **9:00 PM** — evening wind-down
- Adjust to your audience's timezone, not yours

### Music is non-negotiable
- Silent slideshows get buried by the algorithm
- Browse TikTok's sound library for what's trending in beauty/fashion right now
- Takes 30 seconds per post, massive difference in reach
- Check what sounds competitors and trending beauty creators are using

### Captions — long storytelling format
- Structure: Hook sentence > Problem > Discovery > What it does > Result > max 5 hashtags
- Conversational tone, like a text to a friend
- Example: "I've been breaking out for weeks and nothing was working. Then my roommate showed me this app that analyzes your skin type and... okay I'm obsessed? My routine is completely different now and my skin is actually clearing up. Link in bio. #skincare #beautytok #glowup"

### Cross-posting (free extra reach)
- Post the same slideshows to Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, and Threads
- Each platform's algorithm evaluates independently — same content, different audiences

### Consistency > virality
100 okay posts beat 1 viral post. The algorithm rewards regular posting.

## 5. Analytics & The Feedback Loop

### What to track per post (spreadsheet is fine)
- Date posted
- Hook text / hook category (person+conflict, POV, listicle, etc.)
- CTA used ("link in bio", "search on App Store", etc.)
- Views at 48 hours (posts typically peak by then)
- Likes, comments, shares
- Downloads / conversions if you can attribute them

### Diagnostic framework (check weekly)

| Views | Conversions | Diagnosis | Action |
|-------|-------------|-----------|--------|
| High | High | It's working | Make 3 variations of this hook immediately |
| High | Low | Hook works, CTA is broken | Test different CTAs, check your App Store page |
| Low | High | Content converts, nobody sees it | Test radically different hooks, keep the CTA |
| Low | Low | Full reset | New format, new audience angle, research trends |

### Decision rules for hooks
- 50K+ views: DOUBLE DOWN — make 3 variations immediately
- 10K-50K: Good — keep in rotation
- 1K-10K: Try 1 more variation before deciding
- <1K twice: DROP — try something completely different

### CTA rotation (when views are good but conversions aren't)
Cycle through and track which converts best:
1. "Link in bio"
2. "Search [App Name] on the App Store"
3. "[App Name] is free to try — link in bio"
4. Just the app name visible on screen, no explicit CTA
5. "I used [App Name] for this"

### Weekly review ritual
Every Monday morning, review last week's posts:
- Which hook categories got the most views?
- Which CTAs drove actual downloads?
- Double down on winners, drop losers, test 1-2 new hook ideas
